# Requirements — Inbound lead recognition

## User story

As a founder doing founder-led sales, I want the dashboard to surface only
**genuine inbound leads** — people who could become my customer — so that
receipts, SaaS notifications and newsletters from services I subscribe to
never appear as "Hot inbounds" or "Warm leads waiting for a reply".

## Vocabulary

- **Machine-sent**: the message was generated by a system, not typed by a
  human (role local-part like `noreply@`, or an `List-Unsubscribe` /
  `Precedence: bulk` / `Auto-Submitted` header).
- **Two-way conversation**: the contact has at least one outbound activity
  from us AND at least one inbound — a real exchange, not a one-way blast.
- **Unsolicited inbound**: inbound exists but we never sent anything (no
  outbound). Could be a cold human lead OR a vendor/newsletter.
- **Inbound lead**: a human, at an org that fits our ICP, whose relationship
  to us is *prospect* (they could buy from us).

## Acceptance criteria (EARS)

### Deterministic sender classification (pure)

- **R1** WHEN `classifyInboundSender` receives a `fromHeader` whose local-part
  is a role address (`noreply`, `no-reply`, `donotreply`, `notifications`,
  `mailer-daemon`, `bounce`, `postmaster`, `automated`, …) THEN it returns
  `isMachineSent = true` with a `reason` naming the role.
- **R2** WHEN `headers` includes `List-Unsubscribe` or `List-Id` THEN the
  result is `isBulk = true` and `isMachineSent = true` (`senderType =
  automated_marketing`).
- **R3** WHEN `headers` includes `Precedence: bulk|list|junk`,
  `Auto-Submitted` ≠ `no`, or `X-Auto-Response-Suppress` THEN
  `isMachineSent = true` (`senderType = automated_transactional`).
- **R4** WHEN none of the above fire AND the local-part looks personal THEN
  `isMachineSent = false`, `senderType = human`, `reasons = []`.
- **R5** The function is pure: no DB, no network, no ambient clock; identical
  input ⇒ identical output. Header lookups are case-insensitive.

### Capture gating

- **R6** WHEN an inbound email is captured THEN its classification is written
  to `activities.metadata.leadClassification` (`{ senderType, isMachineSent,
  isBulk, reasons, classifier, classifiedAt }`) regardless of outcome — the
  timeline stays complete.
- **R7** WHEN the sender is machine-sent AND no contact already exists for
  that email AND it is not a reply to a tracked thread THEN the system MUST
  NOT auto-create a person-contact and MUST NOT emit `contact/created`
  (so no enrich/qualify/"Hot inbound" fan-out fires for machine mail).
- **R8** WHEN a contact already exists for the sender (known human) THEN the
  email is captured normally even if that single message is automated — the
  machine gate only blocks *creation of new unknown contacts*, never capture
  on an existing entity.
- **R9** WHEN the sender is machine-sent but the company domain already exists
  in the CRM THEN the activity is attached to the **company** (entityType
  `company`), never to a fabricated `Noreply` person.

### Warm-leads surfacing

- **R10** WHEN ranking warm leads THEN a contact whose email is a role address
  (machine-sent) is excluded.
- **R11** WHEN a contact's inbound is **unsolicited** (no outbound from us)
  AND the contact does not clear the ICP-fit floor THEN it is excluded — ICP
  is a **floor**, not a score weight, for unsolicited inbound.
- **R12** WHEN a contact has a **two-way conversation** (≥1 outbound and ≥1
  inbound) THEN it remains eligible regardless of ICP (a real conversation is
  intrinsically warm). Existing recency × depth × ICP ranking is preserved for
  eligible contacts.
- **R13** The existing guards stay: ignored/free domains excluded, zero-inbound
  excluded, 5-min per-tenant cache.

## Edge cases

- Role detection must not false-positive on personal names that *contain* a
  role token (`support.diaz@acme.com` → personal; only the whole local-part or
  a clear `role+tag` shape counts).
- A genuine cold inbound from a human at an in-ICP org (no prior outbound)
  must still surface (passes R11 via the ICP floor).
- Missing `headers` (today's call-sites) must degrade gracefully to role-based
  detection only — never throw (R5).

## Evaluation steps

1. Unit: `classifyInboundSender` truth table (role, List-Unsubscribe,
   Precedence, Auto-Submitted, clean human, header case-insensitivity).
2. Unit: `captureInboundEmail` — machine sender at unknown company creates
   nothing and emits no `contact/created`; known human still captured.
3. Unit: `rankWarmLeads` — role address excluded; unsolicited off-ICP
   excluded; two-way conversation kept; existing ranking unchanged.
4. `tsc` clean, full vitest green (no regression in existing capture /
   warm-leads suites).
