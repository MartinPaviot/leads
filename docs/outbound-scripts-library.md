# B2B Outbound Scripts Library
> Practitioner-grade. Word-for-word ready to use.
> Sources: 30MPC, Jason Bay (Blissful Prospecting), Josh Braun, Lemlist, Sam Blond (Brex/Front), Steli Efti (Close), Kyle Coleman, Gong Labs data.

---

## STAGE 1: COLD EMAIL

---

### 1.1 First-Touch Signal-Triggered

---

**Script 1-A: Post-Funding (Series A/B)**

**Subject:** Quick question after your Series [X]

```
Hi [First Name],

Saw the Series [A/B] announcement — congrats.

One pattern we see often after a round like this: [ICP company type] teams suddenly have budget to fix the [specific pain] they've been duct-taping for 18 months, but the hiring plan absorbs the first 90 days.

We help [1-line ICP description] do [specific outcome] without adding headcount.

Worth 15 minutes before the hiring scramble kicks in?

[Your name]
```

**Mechanism:** Ties your outreach to a verifiable event they just lived, names the exact tension that event creates (cash before team), positions your solution as the bridge.

**Expected performance:** 12-18% reply rate on well-researched lists (Lemlist 2024 benchmark). Signal-triggered emails outperform generic cold by 3-5x (Gong Labs).

**Fails when:** You send it 3+ weeks after the announcement (signal is cold), the funding amount is <$1M (not a buying signal), or you can't map their specific pain to the event.

---

**Script 1-B: Post-Key-Hire (New VP Sales / CRO / CMO)**

**Subject:** [First Name] — one thing the last [role] probably didn't fix

```
Hi [First Name],

Noticed you joined [Company] as [VP Sales / CRO] last month.

Most [VPs/CROs] we talk to inherit three things from their predecessor: a Salesforce instance nobody trusts, a pipeline that's 60% wishful thinking, and a team that's been selling on vibes.

We work with [role]s at [2-3 comparable companies] to [specific outcome in 90 days].

Happy to share what worked for [comparable company] — 15 minutes?

[Your name]
```

**Mechanism:** The new executive is in a 30-90 day window where they're actively diagnosing and fixing inherited problems. You name the exact inherited problems. Zero pitch.

**Expected performance:** 15-22% reply rate. New executives reply at 2x the rate of tenured ones (30MPC internal data). Window closes fast — send within 30 days of LinkedIn start date.

**Fails when:** You send after 60 days (they've made decisions), you name the wrong inherited problems for their industry, or the hire is too junior to have buying authority.

---

**Script 1-C: Post-Event / Post-Conference**

**Subject:** [Conference name] follow-up — [specific session or topic]

```
Hi [First Name],

Were you at [Conference] last week? I caught [specific speaker / session] — the point about [specific insight] landed differently than the usual [topic] take.

It connects directly to something we're seeing with [ICP role] at [company size/type]: [one-sentence problem statement].

I'm putting together a 20-minute roundtable with [3-4 practitioners] who are dealing with this. Would you want a seat?

[Your name]
```

**Mechanism:** Shared context creates immediate rapport. The roundtable ask is lower commitment than a demo and positions you as a convener, not a vendor.

**Expected performance:** 18-25% reply rate when the event is recent (<7 days) and the session reference is specific. Josh Braun calls this "borrowing credibility from the event."

**Fails when:** You didn't actually attend and get caught (they ask a follow-up question you can't answer), the roundtable doesn't actually happen, or you're emailing 2+ weeks post-event.

### 1.2 First-Touch Hyper-Personalized (1:1)

---

**Script 1-D: Deep Research, Single Insight**

**Subject:** [Company name] + [specific observation]

```
Hi [First Name],

I spent 20 minutes on [Company]'s website and noticed [specific observation — pricing page structure, job postings pattern, product changelog language, recent blog post argument].

That usually means [company type] is dealing with [specific inference].

If I'm right, [one sentence on how you help].

Wrong? Tell me what I'm missing — genuinely curious.

[Your name]
```

**Mechanism:** Shows real pre-work. The "tell me what I'm missing" defuses defensiveness and invites a response even if the inference is wrong — Josh Braun's "open question" pattern.

**Expected performance:** 25-40% reply rate on 1:1 campaigns. Not scalable beyond 5-10 per day. Reply quality is significantly higher (prospects often share the real problem unprompted).

**Fails when:** The observation is generic (e.g., "I noticed you're hiring salespeople"), the inference is too aggressive or wrong without any basis, or you're sending at volume (defeats the point).

---

**Script 1-E: LinkedIn Research → Email**

**Subject:** Your post about [topic] — question

```
Hi [First Name],

Your post last week about [specific argument from their LinkedIn post] — specifically the part about [one sentence] — is exactly the tension we see at [ICP company stage].

Most [role]s we talk to resolve it one of two ways: [Option A] or [Option B]. Both have real tradeoffs.

We've been helping [ICP] teams find a third path. Worth 15 minutes to compare notes?

[Your name]
```

**Mechanism:** References their published thinking, not their company. Positions you as someone who engages with ideas, not a vendor who scraped their job title.

**Expected performance:** 20-35% reply. Kyle Coleman's data shows LinkedIn-referenced cold emails get 40% higher open rates and 2x reply rates vs. non-referenced.

**Fails when:** The LinkedIn post is >30 days old, you reference the wrong post (easy to do at scale), or the prospect hasn't posted publicly in months.

### 1.3 First-Touch Broader Cold (With Specificity)

---

**Script 1-F: Problem-Led, ICP Cluster**

**Subject:** [ICP role] at [company stage/size] — quick question

```
Hi [First Name],

I work with [ICP role description] at [company type/stage]. The problem I hear most often: [specific problem in their language, not yours].

Most try to fix it with [common workaround]. It works until [specific failure point].

We do it differently: [one sentence, mechanism not features].

[Comparable company] went from [before] to [after] in [timeframe].

15 minutes to see if it maps to what you're dealing with?

[Your name]
```

**Mechanism:** Pattern-matches against their lived experience using language they would use. The "common workaround → failure point" structure triggers recognition. Sam Blond calls this "speaking their internal monologue."

**Expected performance:** 8-12% reply rate at scale (1000+ contacts). Above industry average (avg cold email reply rate is 1-5%, Lemlist 2024). Works at volume unlike 1-D/1-E.

**Fails when:** The "problem in their language" is wrong for the segment, the comparable company isn't actually comparable to them, or the CTA is too aggressive (asking for a demo rather than a conversation).

---

**Script 1-G: Competitor Customer Targeting**

**Subject:** [Competitor name] user?

```
Hi [First Name],

I noticed [Company] is using [Competitor] — we see that a lot with [ICP type].

The thing that sends them to us is usually [specific limitation of competitor in that use case].

Not saying [Competitor] is wrong for you. Just curious if that's surfaced.

Happy to share how [similar company] made the switch — 15 minutes?

[Your name]
```

**Mechanism:** Shows you did homework (tool stack research via job postings, G2 reviews, LinkedIn). Positions as peer conversation not pitch. "Not saying they're wrong" disarms defensiveness.

**Expected performance:** 10-15% reply rate. Gong Labs found competitor references in subject lines lift open rates 15%. Works best when you have verifiable competitive advantages, not feature parity.

**Fails when:** You're wrong about what tool they use, the "specific limitation" you name doesn't actually affect them, or the competitor has a strong lock-in (switching cost conversation becomes an objection).

### 1.4 Follow-Up #2 — New Angle / New Value

---

**Script 1-H: New Angle, No Guilt**

**Subject:** Re: [original subject] — one more thought

```
Hi [First Name],

Following up on my last note — not going to rehash it.

One thing I didn't mention: [specific new insight, data point, or customer story that's genuinely different from email 1].

[One sentence on why it's relevant to them specifically].

Still worth a quick conversation?

[Your name]
```

**Mechanism:** Explicitly breaks the pattern of guilt-trip follow-ups ("just bumping this..."). Adds new information so the prospect has a reason to re-engage that isn't just social obligation.

**Expected performance:** 5-8% incremental reply rate on non-responders from email 1. 30MPC data shows follow-ups with new information get 3x replies vs. "just following up" messages.

**Fails when:** You don't actually have new information (empty promise), you reference the original email in a way that feels passive-aggressive, or you send it within 48 hours of the first.

---

**Script 1-I: Re-frame the Problem**

**Subject:** Different way to think about [problem]

```
Hi [First Name],

Tried you last week — no worries if it's not the right time.

One reframe that's been useful for [ICP role]s dealing with [problem]: most people think about it as [common framing]. The teams that actually fix it think about it as [alternative framing].

That shift changes where you look for the solution — and who you buy from.

Happy to walk through the logic if useful.

[Your name]
```

**Mechanism:** Josh Braun's "insight-based follow-up." You're not selling harder, you're adding a perspective that makes them think differently. The re-frame becomes the hook, not the product.

**Expected performance:** 6-10% reply rate. Higher quality replies — prospect often shares their current thinking, which is pipeline intelligence.

**Fails when:** The re-frame isn't actually insightful (just wordplay), you're not credible enough to be delivering a perspective shift, or the prospect has already rejected the category.

### 1.5 Follow-Up #3 — Case Study + Social Proof

---

**Script 1-J: Peer Company Story**

**Subject:** How [similar company] handled [problem]

```
Hi [First Name],

Third note — promise I have an actual reason to reach out.

[Similar Company] — [description that makes them feel like a peer: same size, same ICP, same stage, same GTM motion] — was dealing with [specific problem] about 6 months ago.

They tried [thing they tried]. Didn't work because [specific reason].

What actually moved the needle: [specific mechanism, not product feature].

In 90 days they went from [before metric] to [after metric].

Happy to share the full story — 15 minutes?

[Your name]
```

**Mechanism:** Social proof from a peer (not a logo, an actual story with a failure arc). The "tried X, failed because Y" structure builds credibility — it's honest, not a press release.

**Expected performance:** 7-12% reply rate on touch 3. Steli Efti's research at Close: case studies in email 3 get 40% more replies than in email 1 (credibility hasn't been established yet in email 1).

**Fails when:** The "similar company" isn't actually similar (wrong industry, wrong size), the metrics are vague ("significantly improved"), or you use a big-brand logo name that makes them feel inferior rather than inspired.

---

**Script 1-K: ROI-Led**

**Subject:** The [ICP role] math on [problem]

```
Hi [First Name],

Quick math that's been useful for [role]s at [company stage]:

[Problem] typically costs [ICP type]:
- [Line 1: time cost or opportunity cost]
- [Line 2: downstream revenue impact]
- [Line 3: team frustration / retention cost]

At [Company]'s scale, that's roughly [rough estimate in dollars or time].

We typically help recover [specific % or $] of that in [timeframe].

Worth checking the math together?

[Your name]
```

**Mechanism:** Converts an abstract pain into a number. Even if the number is rough, it anchors the conversation on ROI rather than features. Forces the prospect to either agree or correct your math — both are conversations.

**Expected performance:** 8-13% reply rate. Kyle Coleman's data: ROI-framed emails get 35% higher reply rates than feature-framed emails at touch 3.

**Fails when:** The numbers are obviously fabricated or too vague, the prospect's pain point isn't actually quantifiable in a meaningful way, or you haven't established enough credibility for them to trust the math.

### 1.6 Follow-Up #4 — Objection Preempt / Pattern Interrupt

---

**Script 1-L: Objection Preempt**

**Subject:** The thing people usually say at this point

```
Hi [First Name],

You've gotten 3 emails from me. The honest thing to say: I don't know if this is right for you.

Here's what usually comes up at this point:

"We already have [solution]" — fair. We're not [category]. We specifically help when [specific condition].

"Not a priority right now" — also fair. The teams that push back on this are usually dealing with [downstream consequence] in Q[X].

"We're too early / too small" — our smallest customer is [benchmark].

If none of those apply, I'd genuinely like to understand why it's not a fit. Saves us both time.

15 minutes?

[Your name]
```

**Mechanism:** Josh Braun's objection preemption pattern. By raising the objections yourself, you demonstrate confidence and disarm the standard deflection scripts. The "genuinely like to understand why it's not a fit" reframes no-reply as a conversation worth having.

**Expected performance:** 6-10% reply rate. Higher-than-average meeting conversion from replies because self-qualified.

**Fails when:** You raise objections that don't actually apply to their situation, the tone feels presumptuous ("I know what you're thinking"), or you list too many objections (feels like a defensive wall).

---

**Script 1-M: Pattern Interrupt — The Honest Email**

**Subject:** Honest question

```
Hi [First Name],

I've sent you 3 emails. You haven't responded.

One of these is true:
A. My timing is off.
B. You have no interest in [problem category].
C. My emails haven't been good enough to warrant a reply.

If it's A or B — genuinely no problem. Tell me which one and I'll stop.

If it's C — I'd actually appreciate knowing what I missed. I'm trying to get better at this.

[Your name]
```

**Mechanism:** Radical honesty breaks the salesperson-prospect dynamic. Option C inverts the power dynamic — you're asking for feedback, not a meeting. People who would never reply to a pitch often reply to this.

**Expected performance:** 10-18% reply rate (unusually high for touch 4). Meeting conversion from replies is lower, but pipeline quality is high (they tell you the real reason). 30MPC cites this pattern as their highest-performing touch 4.

**Fails when:** The "C" option comes across as passive-aggressive fishing for compliments rather than genuine curiosity, or you're sending to a list where the prospect doesn't remember receiving previous emails.

### 1.7 Break-Up Email (Touch 5-6)

---

**Script 1-N: The Hard Close**

**Subject:** Should I close your file?

```
Hi [First Name],

I've tried reaching you a few times about [one-line problem/solution].

I don't want to keep filling your inbox. Is it safe to assume [problem] isn't something you're focused on right now, and I should close out your file?

If there's a better time to reconnect — quarter change, after [known event] — just let me know and I'll reach back out then.

[Your name]
```

**Mechanism:** Steli Efti's break-up email. The "close your file" framing triggers loss aversion. It's also genuinely respectful — you're giving them an easy out while keeping the door open.

**Expected performance:** 12-20% reply rate — often the highest-reply email in the sequence (people respond to finality). Steli data: break-up emails recover 15-20% of sequences that went silent.

**Fails when:** You've already sent a break-up email earlier in the sequence (loses its power), the "known event" you reference is wrong or too vague, or you send it too early (touch 3-4 is too soon).

---

**Script 1-O: The Value Leave-Behind**

**Subject:** Leaving this with you

```
Hi [First Name],

Last note from me — I don't want to overstay my welcome.

Before I go: [Genuine resource — benchmark report, calculation tool, teardown, framework] that's been useful for [ICP role]s dealing with [problem]. No strings. Just something worth having.

[Link or attachment]

If [problem] becomes a priority later, you know where to find me.

[Your name]
```

**Mechanism:** Ends the sequence with a give, not an ask. Creates reciprocity and leaves a positive final impression. The prospect remembers you as someone who provided value, not someone who pestered them.

**Expected performance:** 5-8% reply rate. 25-30% "saved for later" behavior (they reach out 60-120 days later unprompted). Lemlist data: sequences ending with value assets generate 3x more inbound follow-up vs. sequences ending with another ask.

**Fails when:** The resource isn't actually useful (generic ebook), you're too obviously fishing for a reply ("let me know what you think!"), or you've burned goodwill with aggressive earlier emails.

### 1.8 Re-Engagement After Silence

---

**Script 1-P: Time-Gap Opener**

**Subject:** It's been [X months] — still dealing with [problem]?

```
Hi [First Name],

We connected [X months] ago about [specific problem].

Things change fast. Just curious: is [problem] still on the radar, or has the priority shifted?

If it's moved up the list — we've done some work since we last spoke that might be relevant: [one sentence on what's new].

Happy to pick up the conversation.

[Your name]
```

**Mechanism:** Time-gap re-engagement. The question is genuine and low-pressure. The "things change" framing acknowledges their reality and removes embarrassment from re-engaging.

**Expected performance:** 15-25% reply rate on prospects who previously showed interest. 8-12% on fully cold re-engagement. 30MPC data: re-engagement after 3-6 months outperforms most touch 3-4 of original sequence.

**Fails when:** You re-engage too soon (less than 60 days), the "what's new" item is thin or generic, or there's no actual trigger event to hang the re-engagement on.

---

**Script 1-Q: Product/Company Update Re-Engagement**

**Subject:** [Your company] update — relevant to [problem you discussed]

```
Hi [First Name],

We last connected about [problem/topic].

Since then: [specific, relevant update — new feature, new customer in their space, new data, new integration].

I thought of you specifically because [1-sentence direct connection to their situation].

Worth picking the conversation back up?

[Your name]
```

**Mechanism:** The update gives a genuine, non-salesy reason to reach out. "I thought of you specifically" signals human judgment, not automated blast.

**Expected performance:** 18-28% reply rate on warm re-engagement. Works best when the update is genuinely meaningful to them, not a product release announcement.

**Fails when:** The update isn't relevant (just an excuse to reach out), you can't articulate why you specifically thought of them, or too much time has passed and they no longer remember the original conversation.

## STAGE 2: COLD CALL

### 2.1 Permission-Based Opener

---

**Script 2-A: Jason Bay "Honest Opener"**

```
[Ring. They pick up.]

"Hey [First Name], this is [Your Name] from [Company]. Did I catch you at a terrible time?"

[They say: "No, what's this about?" or "Kind of, what do you need?"]

"I'll be honest — this is a cold call. I'll keep it to 30 seconds and you can tell me if it's worth your time. Deal?"

[They say: yes or laugh — which is also yes]

"[30-second pitch: I work with [ICP] who [specific problem]. We [one-sentence mechanism]. Before I go — is [problem] anything on your radar right now?"
```

**Mechanism:** Jason Bay's permission-based approach (Blissful Prospecting). By calling it a cold call immediately, you eliminate the "gotcha" feeling and build micro-trust in the first 10 seconds. Giving them a 30-second commitment lowers the barrier to listen.

**Expected performance:** 35-50% of prospects who don't hang up will give you the 30 seconds. Connect-to-qualified-conversation rate increases ~40% vs. traditional openers (Gong Labs). Full cold call pickup to conversation: ~8-15%.

**Fails when:** Delivered robotically (the warmth is the mechanism), used with very senior prospects who find "cold call" framing condescending, or when your 30-second pitch is actually 90 seconds.

---

**Script 2-B: Pattern Interrupt Opener**

```
"Hey [First Name], I'll be upfront — you don't know me, this is totally out of the blue. [Your Name] from [Company]."

[Pause]

"I'll take 20 seconds. If it's not relevant, just say the word and I'm gone."

[Pitch]

"[Relevant question that invites their input, not a yes/no]?"
```

**Mechanism:** The pause after "you don't know me" breaks the script they're running in their head ("sales call, deflect"). The 20-second time-box further disarms.

**Expected performance:** Similar to 2-A. Works slightly better with very busy C-suite prospects who respond to directness over warmth.

**Fails when:** The pause is too long (feels awkward over phone), the pitch exceeds the promised time, or the closing question is yes/no (gives them an easy exit).

### 2.2 "Heard the Name Tossed Around" Opener

---

**Script 2-C: Gong Data Winner**

```
"Hey [First Name], this is [Your Name] from [Company]. How've you been?"

[They respond]

"Good to hear. So — I know this is out of the blue. I've actually had your name come up a few times recently in conversations with [vague peer descriptor — 'other sales leaders at growth-stage SaaS companies' / 'a few of your counterparts at Series B companies']. Figured it was worth reaching out directly."

[Brief pause to let them register]

"We work with [ICP] on [problem]. Is that anything you're dealing with right now?"
```

**Mechanism:** Gong Labs analyzed 90,000+ cold calls and found "How've you been?" followed by social proof references had the highest connect-to-meeting rates. The "heard your name" creates social curiosity — people want to know who's talking about them.

**Expected performance:** 10.1% cold call meeting rate (Gong Labs benchmark for top performers) vs. 1-3% average. Note: "How've you been?" to a stranger is controversial — some consider it disingenuous.

**Fails when:** You can't back up the "name tossed around" with anything plausible, used with prospects who see through social engineering (experienced buyers), or delivered without genuine warmth (comes across as manipulation).

### 2.3 Founder-to-Founder Opener

---

**Script 2-D: Peer-to-Peer Direct**

```
"Hey [First Name], [Your Name] here — I'm the founder of [Company]. I'll be quick.

I build [what you build] for founders doing [specific motion]. I called because [specific reason tied to something about them — their company stage, funding round, something they posted].

I'm not trying to sell you anything on this call. I'm genuinely curious if [specific problem] is something you've figured out or still dealing with. 

What's the honest answer?"
```

**Mechanism:** Founder-to-founder calls carry different social weight than SDR-to-prospect calls. "I'm the founder" signals personal investment and peer relationship. The "I'm not trying to sell you anything on this call" is Sam Blond's pattern — removes defensive posture and invites authentic conversation.

**Expected performance:** 20-30% better conversion than SDR calls when the founder has genuine market credibility. Works almost exclusively for early-stage companies where the founder is doing direct sales (0-30 customers). Does not scale.

**Fails when:** The founder doesn't actually make the calls (people can tell), the "specific reason" for calling is too thin, or used once the company has enough brand recognition that the founder shouldn't be cold calling.

### 2.4 Cold Call Objection Handling

---

**Script 2-E: "Not Interested"**

```
Them: "Not interested."

You: "Totally fair — can I ask, is it not interesting because the timing is off, or because it genuinely doesn't apply to what you're dealing with?"

[Pause — let them answer]

[If timing]: "Got it. What would make it the right time — is there a trigger I should look for to reach back out?"

[If doesn't apply]: "What is the [problem] you're actually focused on right now? I want to make sure I understand before I come back."
```

**Mechanism:** Josh Braun's "not interested" recovery. The question separates a timing objection (keep warm) from a genuine rejection (gather intelligence or disqualify). Never argue, never pitch harder — gather information.

**Expected performance:** 20-30% of "not interested" responses are actually timing objections. Recovering even a fraction of these significantly increases qualified pipeline per dial.

**Fails when:** You argue or pitch harder instead of asking, you skip the pause (the silence after the question is where they talk), or you use this on someone who has already clearly rejected the category multiple times.

---

**Script 2-F: "Send Me an Email"**

```
Them: "Just send me an email."

You: "Happy to. Before I do — two quick questions so it's worth your time to open: [Question 1 — most relevant qualifying question]. And [Question 2 — second most relevant]. That way I can make it actually useful instead of generic."

[Take their answers, use them in the email subject line and opener]
```

**Mechanism:** "Send me an email" is the most common deflection and the least intentional one. Instead of treating it as a close, treat it as permission to qualify. The two questions make the email better and establish a micro-commitment (they answered, they're more likely to open).

**Expected performance:** Converts 30-40% of "send an email" deflections into qualified email opens. 30MPC data.

**Fails when:** You ask 3+ questions (they regret giving permission), you ask questions that feel like discovery (they feel tricked into a call), or you send a generic email anyway and waste the permission.

---

**Script 2-G: "We Use [Competitor]"**

```
Them: "We already use [Competitor]."

You: "Makes sense — they're solid. A lot of the teams I work with came from [Competitor]. Can I ask — are you using them for [specific use case A] or more for [specific use case B]?"

[Listen]

"Got it. The reason I ask — where we're different is specifically in [use case they mentioned or implied]. Not trying to replace [Competitor] — but there's a specific gap [similar company] found that's worth knowing about. Worth 10 minutes to look at it?"
```

**Mechanism:** Never badmouth the competitor. Instead, get specific about use case to find the gap. The "not trying to replace" framing lowers defensiveness and positions you as additive.

**Expected performance:** 15-20% conversion on this objection to a conversation. Works best when you have a genuine, specific competitive advantage in a specific use case.

**Fails when:** You don't have a real advantage (you're pitching parity), you badmouth the competitor (triggers loyalty), or the competitor has genuine lock-in that makes switching practically impossible.

---

**Script 2-H: "No Budget"**

```
Them: "We don't have budget for this."

You: "I hear that a lot, and usually when someone says no budget it means one of two things: either it's genuinely not in the plan, or it's not in the plan yet. Which is it for you?"

[If genuinely not in plan]: "Got it. When does your budget cycle open — [Q3/Q4/next fiscal]? Worth keeping in touch so you're not starting from scratch when it does?"

[If hasn't been budgeted yet]: "What would need to be true for you to make a case for this internally? I'd rather help you build the business case than pitch you something you can't buy."
```

**Mechanism:** Steli Efti's budget objection framework. "No budget" is almost never literally true — it's either a priority signal or a discovery that you haven't connected value to cost. The question separates real constraint from unfelt urgency.

**Expected performance:** 25-35% of "no budget" objections reveal it's a prioritization issue (not a real budget constraint), which is solvable with a business case conversation.

**Fails when:** You try to create urgency instead of understanding the real constraint, the company genuinely is in financial distress, or you can't actually help them build a business case.

---

**Script 2-I: "Bad Time / Call Back Later"**

```
Them: "This is a really bad time."

You: "Completely understand. Quick question before I let you go — is it a bad time right now today, or is there a broader thing going on that makes this month a bad time?"

[If just today]: "Got it. What's a better time — literally this week or next week, I'll put it in the calendar now."

[If broader thing]: "Makes sense. What's the trigger that would make this conversation worth having — [quarter close, product launch, budget cycle]? I'll set a reminder and come back then."
```

**Mechanism:** "Call back later" without specificity is a soft rejection. Getting a specific time or trigger converts it from a polite dismissal into a real future touchpoint.

**Expected performance:** Specific callback commitments convert at 40-60% when followed up exactly as committed. Vague "call me next month" callbacks convert at <10% (30MPC).

**Fails when:** You schedule too far out (>2 weeks loses most of the momentum), you don't actually call back at the committed time, or you push for a time when they've clearly indicated a real constraint.

### 2.5 15-Second Voicemail

---

**Script 2-J: Curiosity-Based Voicemail**

```
"Hey [First Name], [Your Name] from [Company]. Quick voicemail — I was reaching out because [one specific reason tied to their situation or a signal]. Curious if [one-sentence question that's genuinely interesting to them]. My number is [number]. [Your Name] from [Company]."

[Total: 12-15 seconds]
```

**Mechanism:** Leave a question, not a pitch. Voicemails that ask a question they want to answer get 3x more callbacks than voicemails that describe your product (Gong Labs). Say your name and company twice — they catch the second one.

**Expected performance:** 4-8% callback rate vs. 1-2% for product-description voicemails. Used as part of a multi-touch sequence (email + call + voicemail), not standalone.

**Fails when:** The question is generic ("are you interested in increasing revenue?"), the voicemail is >20 seconds (they stop listening), or you don't leave your number clearly.

---

**Script 2-K: Pattern Interrupt Voicemail**

```
"Hey [First Name], [Your Name] — [Company]. This is a cold call voicemail, which means you're probably going to delete this. Before you do: [one-sentence insight or question that's genuinely relevant]. That's it. [Number]. [Your Name]."
```

**Mechanism:** The "you're probably going to delete this" meta-awareness disarms the "it's a sales voicemail" filter. The insight has to be genuinely interesting — this pattern has no margin for generic content.

**Expected performance:** 6-10% callback rate when the insight is sharp. Higher open rate but more variable than 2-J.

**Fails when:** The insight is weak (the entire script is built on the insight quality), or the self-aware framing comes across as gimmicky rather than confident.

## STAGE 3: LINKEDIN

---

### 3.1 Connection Request

---

**Script 3-A: Signal-Based, No Pitch**

```
"[First Name] — saw your post about [specific topic]. [One-sentence genuine reaction]. Would be good to connect."

[118 characters]
```

**Mechanism:** References specific content they created. Zero pitch. The constraint (150 chars) enforces no-pitch discipline. Works because it demonstrates you read their content.

**Expected performance:** 35-50% connection acceptance rate (vs. 10-20% for generic "I'd like to connect"). Kyle Coleman's LinkedIn benchmark.

**Fails when:** The post reference is vague ("great content!"), you're sending to people whose content you haven't actually read, or you add any form of pitch.

---

**Script 3-B: Shared Context**

```
"[First Name] — we were both at [Event] / both in [Group] / both connected to [Mutual]. Figured worth connecting."

[Under 130 characters]
```

**Mechanism:** Shared context is the fastest trust signal. No pitch, no CTA, no ask. The connection itself is the first step.

**Expected performance:** 40-55% acceptance rate when context is accurate and recent. Falls to 15-20% when context is old or tangential.

**Fails when:** The shared context is thin or manufactured, you add "I wanted to reach out about..." at the end (breaks the pattern), or the mutual connection is not actually a meaningful shared contact.

---

**Script 3-C: Role/Company Trigger**

```
"[First Name] — just saw you joined [Company] as [Role]. Congrats. Would be good to have you in my network — a lot of [ICP peers] in here."

[Under 150 characters]
```

**Mechanism:** Acknowledges a real event in their professional life. The "lot of peers" framing positions your network as valuable to them, not just you wanting their connection.

**Expected performance:** 30-45% acceptance. Strongest in first 30 days after role change.

**Fails when:** You send it 60+ days after the role change (the congratulation is stale), or you add any commercial angle to the request.

---

### 3.2 Post-Connection First DM

---

**Script 3-D: Curiosity, No Ask (Xenia Pattern)**

```
"[First Name] — thanks for connecting.

I noticed [specific thing from their profile, post, or company] and had a genuine question: [specific, open-ended question that's interesting to them — not a qualifying question for you].

No agenda — just something I've been thinking about in [their space]."
```

**Mechanism:** Named after Xenia Muntean's LinkedIn method. First message is a genuine question, no CTA, no soft pitch. The goal is to start a conversation about their world, not to qualify them. People who reply have demonstrated willingness to engage.

**Expected performance:** 25-40% reply rate on first DM (vs. 5-10% for pitch DMs). Lower immediate conversion to meeting, but conversation quality and eventual conversion to pipeline is 2-3x higher (30MPC LinkedIn data).

**Fails when:** The question is actually a disguised qualifier ("What tools do you use for X?"), you pivot to pitch in the second message, or the question is so broad it requires an essay to answer.

---

**Script 3-E: Value First, No Ask**

```
"[First Name] — glad to be connected.

Saw you're working on [problem area]. We published something relevant last week: [link to genuinely useful content — benchmark, framework, teardown].

Worth 5 minutes if [problem] is on your radar. No reply needed."
```

**Mechanism:** "No reply needed" removes the social obligation and actually increases replies (paradox of no-ask). The resource has to be genuinely useful — this is a brand-building move, not a lead gen play.

**Expected performance:** 15-25% reply rate. 35-50% click rate on the resource. Primary value is positioning yourself as a useful source, not just a vendor.

**Fails when:** The content is gated or requires signup, the content isn't genuinely relevant to them, or "no reply needed" is followed immediately by a CTA.

---

### 3.3 Comment That Opens Dialogue

---

**Script 3-F: Disagree Respectfully**

```
"Interesting take. I've seen the opposite in [specific context] — [1-2 sentences on your counterpoint]. Curious if you're seeing [question that invites their perspective]."
```

**Mechanism:** Respectful disagreement generates more engagement than agreement. The question at the end invites them to respond to you specifically. Josh Braun: "comments that create tension get 4x more replies than complimentary comments."

**Expected performance:** 30-50% reply rate from post author. High visibility — LinkedIn algorithm surfaces engaged comments to other followers, amplifying reach.

**Fails when:** The disagreement is disrespectful or dismissive, you're wrong about the counterpoint, or you use this formula obviously (feels like a script).

---

**Script 3-G: Specific Additive Insight**

```
"This. Especially [specific point from their post]. One thing I'd add from what I've seen with [specific cohort]: [1-2 sentence insight that's genuinely additive, not just agreement]. Does [question about their experience] factor into your thinking here?"
```

**Mechanism:** Validates their point specifically (not generically), adds unique value, invites a direct reply. The specificity of the validation shows you actually read the post.

**Expected performance:** 25-40% reply rate from post author. Strong for building a long-term relationship before any commercial conversation.

**Fails when:** "This." as an opener without the specific follow-through is empty, the additive insight is actually just repetition of what they said, or you're commenting on posts from people you've already been pitching.

## STAGE 4: DISCOVERY / DEMO

---

### 4.1 Pre-Meeting Qualification Email

---

**Script 4-A: 24-Hour Pre-Meeting Email**

**Subject:** [First Name] — quick prep for tomorrow

```
Hi [First Name],

Looking forward to our call tomorrow at [time].

To make sure the 30 minutes is worth your time:

1. [One sentence on what you'll cover — and what you won't]: "I'll focus on [specific use case] vs. giving you a product walkthrough. If there's another area you'd rather explore, let me know."

2. [One honest expectation]: "We're not the right fit if [clear disqualifier]. I'd rather know upfront."

3. [One question to prep them]: "If you can have a rough answer to [one qualifying question] — it'll make the conversation much more useful."

See you tomorrow.

[Your name]
```

**Mechanism:** Sam Blond's pre-call email pattern. Sets agenda, sets honest expectations, gives them one thing to prepare. The disqualifier signals confidence and filters out mismatched prospects before they consume demo resources.

**Expected performance:** 15-20% reduction in no-shows. 25-30% improvement in discovery call quality (prospect arrives prepared with context). Steli Efti cites this as one of the highest-ROI emails in a sales sequence.

**Fails when:** The disqualifier isn't real (you take all comers), the question is too complex to answer in a day, or it arrives less than 12 hours before the call.

---

### 4.2 Discovery Opener

---

**Script 4-B: Set the Frame Before the Questions**

```
"Before we dive in — let me tell you what I'm hoping to get out of this call, and you can tell me if that's right for you.

My goal is to understand whether what we do actually maps to what you're dealing with. Not to pitch you. If it doesn't map — I'll tell you that, and I won't waste your time.

Does that work?

[Yes]

Great. So — [Name], walk me back to where this [problem/initiative] started. What triggered the conversation internally?"
```

**Mechanism:** Explicit framing removes the "this is a pitch, I need my defenses up" posture. The "I'll tell you if it doesn't map" builds trust. The open-ended "walk me back" question gets the origin story, which is where the real pain and urgency live.

**Expected performance:** Discovery calls with explicit "here's my agenda" openers have 33% higher close rates (Gong Labs: calls where reps set the agenda upfront outperform those that don't by 36%). Not a metric you'll see without attribution.

**Fails when:** You set the frame but then immediately pitch, you don't actually follow through on "I'll tell you if it doesn't map," or you skip to a scripted question list without listening to the origin story.

---

**Script 4-C: The Pain-First Discovery**

```
"[Name] — before I ask you a bunch of questions, can I share what brought you to the call in the first place based on what I know, and you can tell me if I have it right?

[State what you know about their situation based on research]

How close is that?

[They correct or confirm — this is gold]

Tell me more about the [piece they corrected or confirmed]. Specifically — what does it cost you when that goes wrong?"
```

**Mechanism:** Demonstrating research before asking questions establishes credibility and shifts the dynamic from interrogation to collaboration. The "how close is that?" question is Josh Braun's technique — it invites correction, which gives you the real picture.

**Expected performance:** Significantly higher quality discovery data. Prospects who feel understood (vs. interrogated) share 40% more information in the same call time (Gong Labs speaker ratio data — top performers talk 43-44% of the time).

**Fails when:** Your pre-call research is thin (you're guessing and get it wrong), you don't actually listen to the correction and plow ahead, or you use this with very senior prospects who expect you to ask good questions, not assume.

---

### 4.3 Demo Opener

---

**Script 4-D: Set Agenda, Get Buy-In**

```
"Before I show you anything — I want to make sure we cover what matters to you, not what I normally show.

Based on our last call / your pre-call email, you mentioned [3 specific things]:
1. [Problem/goal 1]
2. [Problem/goal 2]
3. [Question they raised]

I'll orient the demo around those. At the end, I want to hear your honest take — whether it solves those or doesn't.

Does that sound right? Anything you'd add or change?"
```

**Mechanism:** Sam Blond's demo framework. By orienting the demo around their stated priorities (not your feature tour), you increase relevance and buy-in. "Whether it solves those or doesn't" signals confidence without overselling.

**Expected performance:** Demo-to-next-step conversion increases 20-30% when the demo is anchored to pre-stated priorities (Gong Labs: top-performing demos have a clear agenda confirmed by the prospect at the start).

**Fails when:** You don't have enough pre-call information to set a real agenda, you deviate from the agenda mid-demo to show "one more thing," or you ask for their honest take and then argue with their honest assessment.

---

### 4.4 Demo Close + Next Steps

---

**Script 4-E: The Specific Next Step Close**

```
"Based on what you saw today — what's your reaction? [Pause and listen without filling the silence]

[Positive reaction]

Good. What I typically suggest as a next step is [specific option, not open-ended]: [Option A: narrow-scope pilot with specific criteria / Option B: technical eval with [specific person] / Option C: bring in [stakeholder] for a second conversation].

Which of those makes sense given where you are internally?

[Let them choose or redirect]

Let's put a specific date on it now — [day/time]. What works for you?"
```

**Mechanism:** Steli Efti's next-step framework. Never leave a demo without a specific next step committed. The choice between concrete options (not "what would you like to do next?") forces a decision without being pushy.

**Expected performance:** Demo-to-opportunity conversion rate increases 2-3x when the specific next step is agreed on the call (vs. "I'll follow up"). Steli's data: deals with defined next steps at every stage close at 2.4x the rate of deals without.

**Fails when:** You skip the pause and answer for them, you offer an open-ended "what would you like to do next?", or you accept "let me think about it" without getting a specific date or trigger event.

## STAGE 5: CLOSING

---

### 5.1 Mutual Action Plan Email

---

**Script 5-A: Mutual Close Plan**

**Subject:** [Company A] + [Company B] — close plan

```
Hi [First Name],

Following up on our conversation — I wanted to put together a shared view of what it takes to get from here to a decision.

CLOSE PLAN — [Your Company] + [Their Company]

Goal: [Outcome they stated in discovery — their language, not yours]
Decision date: [Date they gave or you're proposing]
Decision maker(s): [Names + roles confirmed]

Steps:

Week 1: [Step — e.g., technical review with [Name], security questionnaire]
Week 2: [Step — e.g., reference call with [customer name]]
Week 3: [Step — e.g., commercial review with [CFO/VP Fin]]
Week 4: [Step — Contract execution / go-live planning]

On our side, I'll handle: [Your commitments]
On your side, we'll need: [Their commitments]

Let me know if any of this is off — I'd rather adjust the plan than have it be a surprise on either end.

[Your name]
```

**Mechanism:** Sam Blond's mutual close plan. Collaborative document forces both sides to commit to a timeline. The "let me know if this is off" framing makes it feel like a working document, not a sales pressure play. Surfaces hidden stakeholders (legal, security, finance) before they blow up the deal.

**Expected performance:** Deals with a mutual action plan close 25-35% faster than deals without (Sam Blond at Brex, cited in multiple interviews). Forecast accuracy increases because you've aligned on criteria.

**Fails when:** You push the plan on a prospect who hasn't confirmed they're serious (too early), the plan is one-sided (all their commitments, none of yours), or you create it without involving them (they don't feel ownership).

---

### 5.2 Verbal-Yes Follow-Up

---

**Script 5-B: Verbal Yes → Contract**

**Subject:** Great call — next steps to make it official

```
Hi [First Name],

Really good conversation today. Here's where we landed:

Decision: Move forward with [specific package/scope]
Start date: [Date]
Contract: I'll send via [DocuSign/PandaDoc] in the next [2 hours / by EOD]

Two things I need from you:
1. Confirm the billing contact: [name/email]
2. Confirm the legal entity name for the contract: [Company legal name]

I'll hold [onboarding slot] for you — [date/time] with [name].

Looking forward to getting started.

[Your name]
```

**Mechanism:** Steli Efti's verbal-yes protocol. A verbal yes is not a deal. Move immediately to concrete next steps: contract sent same day, onboarding slot held, billing/legal details confirmed. The specificity prevents drift.

**Expected performance:** Verbal-yes to signature within 24 hours closes at 70-80%. Verbal-yes without same-day contract drops to 40-50% within 7 days (Steli Efti, Close). Each day of delay reduces close probability by ~8%.

**Fails when:** You wait 24+ hours to send the contract, you don't hold a real onboarding slot (just a formality), or you don't get the billing/legal details upfront and let them create an excuse to revisit the decision.

---

### 5.3 "Closing the File" / Break-Up

---

**Script 5-C: Deal Break-Up**

**Subject:** Closing [Company] + [Their Company] opportunity

```
Hi [First Name],

I haven't heard back since [last touchpoint]. I don't want to keep pinging you, so I'm going to close out this opportunity on our end.

If timing was the issue — I'm happy to revisit in [Q3 / after [event]].

If the deal isn't going forward, I'd genuinely appreciate knowing why. Not to re-open it — just to learn.

Either way, no hard feelings.

[Your name]
```

**Mechanism:** Steli's "closing the file" late-stage break-up. Loss aversion triggers a response. The offer to revisit later keeps the relationship intact. The "I'd appreciate knowing why" often gets the real objection you never got during the deal.

**Expected performance:** 20-30% response rate after silence. 10-15% of responses result in re-opened deals. Remaining responses provide competitive intelligence (why you lost).

**Fails when:** Sent too early (before you've been following up for 2+ weeks), or the tone reads as punishing them rather than genuinely closing with respect.

---

**Script 5-D: The "Honest Conversation" Close**

```
"[Name], I want to be direct. 

We've been talking for [X weeks]. I think there's a real fit here. But I also get the sense something's holding this up.

Is it something I haven't addressed? Or is there something internally that makes this hard to move forward right now?

I'd rather have that conversation than keep doing the follow-up dance."
```

**Mechanism:** Verbal version of the break-up email. Surfaces hidden objections that are killing a deal in committee. "The follow-up dance" names their experience and shows self-awareness.

**Expected performance:** Surfaces the real objection in 40-60% of stalled deals. Conversion from "real objection surfaced" to closed deal: 30-40%. Kyle Coleman's late-stage deal recovery technique.

**Fails when:** You ask the question and then immediately try to solve their answer (they stop being honest), or you use it too early and come across as desperate rather than confident.

---

### 5.4 Renewal Conversation Opener

---

**Script 5-E: 90 Days Out Renewal**

**Subject:** [Company] + [Your Company] — 90-day conversation

```
Hi [First Name],

Your renewal is coming up in [month] — 90 days out. I want to get ahead of it rather than have it be a surprise.

Before we talk numbers, I want to make sure we've earned it. A few things I want to cover:

1. What's working well and what we should double down on
2. Anything that hasn't delivered what you expected
3. What's changed in your business that affects how you use [product]

I'd rather hear the hard stuff now than at renewal time.

Can we get [30 minutes] on the calendar before [date]? I'll come with data on your usage/outcomes on our side.

[Your name]
```

**Mechanism:** Proactive renewal conversations 90 days out convert at 2-3x the rate of reactive conversations at 30 days out (Gainsight benchmark). Leading with "did we earn it" shifts from contract negotiation to value conversation.

**Expected performance:** 80-90% of proactively managed renewals close (vs. 60-70% reactive). Net revenue retention improvement of 10-15% on accounts where this process is followed (Gainsight 2023 report).

**Fails when:** You've had no meaningful contact with the account in the preceding 6 months (you don't have the relationship equity), the "what's not working" conversation surfaces serious issues you haven't been aware of, or you come without prepared data on their usage.

---

**Script 5-F: At-Risk Renewal**

**Subject:** Honest conversation about your renewal

```
Hi [First Name],

I want to be upfront: I know [product/account] hasn't performed the way we both hoped this year.

I've been looking at [specific usage data / outcome metrics]. [One-sentence honest assessment of what didn't deliver].

Here's what I propose: let's get on a call this week. I'll come with a clear plan for what we'd do differently in year 2 — and if we can't make the case that it's worth renewing, I'll tell you that.

[Your name]
```

**Mechanism:** Proactive acknowledgment of failure before the prospect raises it. Builds trust and removes the defensive posture from the renewal conversation. "I'll tell you that" signals confidence over desperation.

**Expected performance:** At-risk accounts contacted proactively with honest acknowledgment of problems retain at 40-50% vs. 15-20% for at-risk accounts discovered at renewal (Gainsight).

**Fails when:** You don't actually have a credible plan for year 2, you acknowledge the problem but then pivot immediately to commercial terms, or you send this without actually looking at their usage data first.

### 5.5 Referral Request Post-Value

---

**Script 5-G: Specific Referral Ask**

**Subject:** Quick ask — one name

```
Hi [First Name],

Really happy with how [specific milestone or outcome] came together.

I have one ask: is there one person at [peer company type] — not a dozen, just one — who you think would get value from a conversation like the one we had?

You don't have to intro us if you'd rather not. Even just a name is helpful.

[Your name]
```

**Mechanism:** "One name, not a dozen" removes the cognitive load of generating a referral list. The specific milestone anchors the ask to a moment of value, not an arbitrary ask. "You don't have to intro us" reduces the social risk of giving a name.

**Expected performance:** 30-40% response rate when asked at a specific value moment. Referred leads close at 3-5x the rate of cold outreach (Sam Blond at Brex cited 3x). Most referred leads convert to meetings at 60-70% vs. cold outreach at 3-8%.

**Fails when:** You ask before a clear value moment has been delivered, you ask for multiple referrals at once, or you ask someone who is still internally lukewarm on you.

---

**Script 5-H: Warm Introduction Request**

**Subject:** [First Name], intro to [Specific Name at Company]?

```
Hi [First Name],

I saw you're connected to [Specific Person] at [Company]. I've been trying to reach them about [1-sentence reason that's relevant to them, not just your pipeline].

Would you be comfortable making a quick intro? Happy to draft the note if it makes it easier — just say the word.

[Your name]
```

**Mechanism:** Specific — one name, one company, one reason. "Happy to draft the note" removes the friction barrier. People are far more likely to make an intro if the work is already done.

**Expected performance:** 40-60% of specific intro requests are fulfilled vs. 5-10% of generic "who do you know" requests. Referred intros convert to meetings at 60-80%.

**Fails when:** You ask for intros to multiple people at once, the reason you want the intro is obviously self-interested without being valuable to the person being referred, or you ask someone you haven't built genuine rapport with.

## USAGE NOTES

**Sequencing logic:**
- Touches 1-3: Problem-led, new information each time, no guilt
- Touch 4: Pattern interrupt or objection preempt
- Touch 5-6: Break-up (never before touch 5)
- Spacing: Day 1 → Day 3 → Day 7 → Day 14 → Day 21 → Day 35

**Personalization floor:**
- Signal-triggered: minimum 1 verifiable signal + 1 inference
- Hyper-personalized: minimum 20 minutes of research, 3+ specific observations
- Broader cold: minimum ICP cluster accuracy (right role, right company type, right pain)

**A/B testing priorities (in order):**
1. Subject line (open rate)
2. Opening line (read rate)
3. CTA (reply rate)
4. Length (engagement rate)

**What not to test simultaneously:** Never A/B more than one variable per sequence.

**Channel sequencing (30MPC recommended):**
- Day 1: Email
- Day 3: Call + voicemail
- Day 5: LinkedIn connection
- Day 7: Email
- Day 10: Call (no voicemail)
- Day 14: Email (new angle)
- Day 21: LinkedIn DM
- Day 28: Email (case study)
- Day 35: Break-up
